Machine learning for glacial seismology

One of the most dire consequences of a warming climate is the melting glaciers and ice sheets, which is leading to sea level rise and interruptions to fresh water sources. My research group is developing ways to constantly monitor large ice bodies in real-time by combining seismology (the study of vibrations in the ground) with recent advances in machine learning. Check out our latest project using a novel machine learning algorithm called “SpecUFEx” (Spectral Unsupervised Feature Extraction) to reveal seismic signals related to water flowing about the second largest glacier in Switzerland, Gornergletscher.

Monitoring volcanoes with AI

For most people, volcanic eruptions are a far-flung and exotic threat, yet over 80 million people across the globe live in constant danger of active volcanoes, and scores of tourists flock to experience them every year. I work with a multidisciplinary team of volcanologists at Lamont who are dedicated to anticipating these threats in order to reduce volcanic risks and keep people safe. My work is using data science and machine learning to analyze seismic data from the volcanoes. Read more about Lamont Doherty’s AVERT initiative (Anticipating Volcanic Eruptions in Real-Time), and check out my chat with National Geographic on whether we should really worry about earthquakes triggering large volcanic eruptions.
